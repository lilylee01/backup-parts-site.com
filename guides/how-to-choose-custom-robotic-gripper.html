How to Choose a Custom Robotic Gripper for Irregular-Shaped Parts

In most automation projects, irregular-shaped parts cannot be handled reliably by standard parallel grippers. When the contact surface is unstable or the center of gravity is inconsistent, a custom robotic gripper is usually required.

Many production issues such as slippage, misalignment, dropped parts, and unstable pick-and-place motions are not caused by robot accuracy, but by improper gripping design.

Key Factors to Consider

1. Contact Points

Irregular parts often have limited or uneven gripping surfaces. A custom gripper must be designed to match the actual geometry of the part rather than forcing a generic shape to work.

In many cases, using contoured jaws, adaptive fingers, or soft contact materials significantly improves stability.

2. Center of Gravity

If the center of gravity is off-axis or shifts between parts, standard grippers may cause tilting or rotation during motion. A custom design allows engineers to position the gripping points to better balance the load.

3. Surface Condition

Oily, smooth, flexible, or polished surfaces reduce friction and increase the risk of slippage. In these cases, gripping force alone is not enough â€” surface-adapted materials or vacuum-assisted designs may be required.

4. Payload Margin

A safety margin is critical in industrial automation. In most applications, a payload margin of at least 30% is recommended to compensate for dynamic forces, wear, and variations.

5. Motion Speed and Acceleration

High-speed pick-and-place operations introduce strong inertial forces. Even lightweight parts can slip or deform under sudden acceleration. A custom gripper design can distribute forces more evenly and reduce shock loads.

When a Custom Gripper Is Required

A custom gripper is usually the best option when two or more of the following conditions are present:

  • Irregular or asymmetric geometry
  • Unstable or shifting center of gravity
  • Fragile or deformable materials
  • Oily, dusty, or reflective surfaces
  • High-speed or high-acceleration motion

Standard grippers are designed for flexibility, not optimization. In real production environments, reliability is often more important than general-purpose compatibility.

Why Many Manufacturers Choose Custom Grippers

Custom grippers reduce scrap rates, minimize downtime, and improve repeatability. They also make automation more predictable, especially in environments where part geometry or surface conditions vary.

Instead of adapting the process to the gripper, a custom solution adapts the gripper to the process.

Many OEMs and system integrators prefer custom gripping solutions when standard models fail to meet stability or accuracy requirements.
